Why does the extension need microphone permission?

The microphone permission is required to list audio output devices. The microphone is never used to capture or record any audio — it is only consulted to read the names of your output devices.

Why this is necessary

Browsers require microphone permission to access the list of audio devices (speakers, headphones, etc.). This is a browser security requirement, not a choice by the extension.

  • Without permission: Only "Default" device available
  • With permission: All your audio devices appear in the dropdown

Privacy note

The extension only calls navigator.mediaDevices.enumerateDevices() to get device names. No microphone audio stream is ever created, recorded, or transmitted.

Tab audio can be recorded via the separate Recording feature (the ● button in the popup volume row), which uses Tab Capture rather than the microphone. Recordings are saved to your local Downloads folder and never leave your device. See the Recording question below for details.

Why am I seeing multiple permission prompts?

Some websites use iframes that each request permission separately.

Why this happens

  • The site loads games/widgets in separate embedded frames (iframes)
  • Each iframe is treated as a separate context by the browser
  • Each context needs its own permission grant

What to do

You can safely accept these prompts. The extension only uses the permission to list output devices for the device switcher dropdown.

Why does perceived volume change when I switch devices?

The extension's volume slider stays the same — what changes is how loud it sounds through different hardware.

Why this happens

  • Each device has its own OS volume level (speakers may be at 50%, headphones at 100%)
  • Different hardware has different amplifier power and driver sensitivity
  • External devices (Bluetooth, USB) may have their own hardware volume on top of the OS level

How to adjust

Adjust the device's volume in your OS sound settings, or use the extension's volume slider to compensate.

Why does volume sound different when audio processing is disabled?

This is expected behavior due to how audio is processed differently.

With audio processing enabled

Audio flows through a processing pipeline:

  • Tab audio is captured at the browser level via Tab Capture
  • Routed through Web Audio API (MediaStreamSource → GainNode → Limiter → Output)
  • Even at 100% volume (gain = 1.0), this routing introduces slight signal attenuation

Note: This processing chain applies when Tab Capture is active. When a tab is dormant (not yet activated), audio plays natively without any processing.

With audio processing disabled

Audio goes directly to speakers:

  • Uses the browser's native element.volume property
  • No AudioContext routing or processing
  • Direct path = louder perceived volume at the same slider position

Tip

When toggling audio processing, you may need to adjust the volume slider slightly to match your preferred loudness level.

Why do I need to click the extension for each tab?

Chrome requires a "user gesture" before Tab Capture can access a tab's audio. This is a browser security feature.

Why this requirement exists

Chrome prevents extensions from silently capturing audio without your knowledge. By requiring a user gesture (clicking the extension icon or using a keyboard shortcut), Chrome ensures you've consciously granted permission.

How to know when Tab Capture needs enabling

Look for the blue ! badge on the extension icon — this indicates Tab Capture isn't active on the current tab yet. Click the extension or use a keyboard shortcut to enable it. (See the badge question below for all badge types.)

How it works

  • Opening the popup while a tab is active grants Tab Capture permission for that tab
  • Once activated, Tab Capture stays active on that tab until you close it
  • You can switch to other tabs freely — Tab Capture keeps running in the background
  • Using a keyboard shortcut (e.g., Alt+Shift+Up) on a dormant tab will also activate Tab Capture without opening the popup
  • Your volume, bass, treble, and other settings persist on that tab

Tip

You only need to open the popup once per tab. After that, Tab Capture remains active even when the popup is closed, and the blue ! badge will disappear.

What do the colored ! badges on the extension icon mean?

The extension icon shows different colored ! badges to indicate the current state. These are checked in priority order (highest first).

Yellow ! — Restricted page (highest priority)

The extension can't run on this page type. This appears on browser-internal pages like chrome://, about:, extension pages, and browser stores. Audio control isn't available on these pages.

Red ! — Site rule pending

A saved site rule is waiting to be applied. On Chrome, this appears when a site rule exists for the current domain but Tab Capture hasn't been activated yet. Click the extension icon to apply the rule.

Blue ! — Tab Capture pending

Tab Capture pending: The tab's audio mode requires Tab Capture, which needs a user gesture to activate. Open the popup or use a keyboard shortcut to start. Once activated, the badge changes to the normal volume display.

Grey ! — Dormant (lowest priority)

The tab is dormant — audio processing is inactive. This appears when you've opened the popup on a tab but haven't changed any settings. Change a setting (volume, effects, etc.) or use a keyboard shortcut to activate the tab.

Volume percentage badge (normal state)

When none of the above conditions apply, the icon shows the current tab's volume percentage. The badge color depends on your badge style setting in Settings:

  • Light on Dark — White text on black background (default)
  • Dark on Light — Black text on white background
  • Volume Color — Color changes with volume level (blue → green → yellow → orange → purple)

At 0% (muted), the badge always turns red regardless of style.

What is dormant mode?

The extension stays dormant (inactive) on every tab until you interact with it. This prevents interference with page audio on sites you haven't touched.

Why dormant mode exists

Without dormant mode, the extension would intercept audio on every page load, which could cause issues like volume slider snap-back on sites like YouTube. Dormant mode ensures the extension only touches audio when you want it to.

How tabs activate

  • Changing a setting — Adjusting volume, effects, or any control in the popup activates the tab
  • Keyboard shortcuts — Using volume up/down or mute activates the tab
  • Site rules — Tabs with matching site rules show a red ! badge and activate when you click the extension

Simply opening the popup does not activate a tab — you need to actually change a setting.

What happens when dormant

  • A grey ! badge appears on the extension icon (only visible after opening the popup without changing settings — by default tabs show a blue ! indicating Tab Capture is pending)
  • Page audio works exactly as it normally would
  • No audio processing chain is created
  • Zero impact on page performance or audio behavior

What settings sync across my devices?

Most settings sync automatically when signed into your browser account.

Synced settings

  • All preset values (volume, bass, treble, voice, speed, balance, volume steps, sleep timer)
  • Site rules and disabled domains list
  • Audio mode overrides (Enabled and Disabled sites)
  • Default audio mode
  • Theme, visualizer style/color, EQ mode, Basic/Advanced mode, badge style
  • Header layout and popup sections customization
  • Visibility preferences (visualizer, seekbar, shortcuts footer)
  • Seekbar time display (total/remaining), tab info location
  • UI state (expanded/collapsed sections)

Local only

  • Default audio device (different hardware per device)
  • Current tab volumes (session-specific)

Note

Sync only works within the same browser. Chrome syncs to Chrome, etc. Cross-browser sync is not possible.

How do I reset everything to defaults?

There are several ways to reset settings, depending on what you need.

Popup reset controls

  • Reset current tab — Click the ↺ button in the header area. Resets volume, balance, channel mode, all effects, and output device to defaults.
  • Reset balance — Click the ⟲ button next to the balance slider
  • Reset individual effects — Click the ⟲ button next to each effect slider (speed, bass, treble, voice, range)

Settings page reset controls

  • Reset individual sections — Use "Reset to Defaults" buttons in each Settings section
  • Reset everything — Use "Reset All Settings" in the Data Management section

What "Reset All Settings" clears

  • All preset values back to defaults
  • All site rules and disabled domains deleted
  • All audio mode overrides cleared (Enabled and Disabled sites)
  • Default audio mode reset
  • Theme, visualizer style/color, EQ mode, Basic/Advanced mode, badge style reset to defaults
  • Header layout and popup sections reset to default
  • Visibility preferences reset (visualizer, seekbar, shortcuts footer)
  • Seekbar time display, tab info location reset to defaults
  • Default audio device setting cleared

Note: Keyboard shortcuts and microphone permissions are managed by your browser. See Settings → Browser Settings to manage these.

How do I customize the popup header?

There are two ways to customize which icons appear in the popup header and their order:

  • Quick Edit Mode — Long-press (hold for ~1 second) the ABTC logo in the popup. Icons will wiggle, and you can drag them to reorder, show/hide icons, and adjust spacers.
  • Settings Page — Open Settings → Header Layout for the full drag-and-drop editor with a preview.

Both methods save changes instantly and sync with each other in real-time.

Does the extension have keyboard shortcuts?

Yes — three global keyboard shortcuts work from any tab without opening the popup.

Default shortcuts

  • Alt+Shift+Up — Increase volume
  • Alt+Shift+Down — Decrease volume
  • Alt+Shift+M — Toggle mute

Customizing shortcuts

  • Go to chrome://extensions/shortcuts

You can change these to any key combination that doesn't conflict with browser or system shortcuts.

Does the extension work on Spotify, Netflix, and other DRM sites?

Yes. Tab Capture captures audio at the browser level, so DRM restrictions don't affect it. Volume boost, effects, and visualizer all work.

Playback controls

Play/pause and seeking work on Spotify and similar sites by interacting with the site's own UI controls, since the underlying audio elements may not be directly accessible.

Does the extension collect any data?

No. The extension has no analytics, no tracking, and makes no external network requests.

What stays local

  • All settings are stored in your browser's local and sync storage
  • No data is ever sent to external servers
  • No third-party libraries, scripts, or dependencies
  • Microphone permission is only used to list audio output devices — no microphone audio is ever captured or transmitted
  • Tab audio recordings (when you opt in via the Record button) are saved to your local Downloads folder and never leave your device

Sync storage

If you're signed into your browser, settings sync through your browser account (Chrome Sync). This is handled entirely by the browser — the extension doesn't manage or access your account.

What is Active Tab Audio (Focus Mode)?

Focus Mode automatically mutes all tabs except the one you're viewing.

How it works

When enabled via the header toggle, audio follows your active tab — switching tabs instantly mutes the previous tab and unmutes the new one. This is useful when you have multiple tabs playing audio and want to quickly switch between them without manually muting each one.

How to use

Toggle it on/off using the focus icon in the popup header.

What is the sleep timer?

The sleep timer lets you schedule audio to automatically pause after a set duration.

How it works

Available in Advanced mode, the sleep timer pauses audio after a preset duration. Choose 5, 15, 30, or 60 minutes (each preset is customizable in Settings → Presets → Sleep Timer, supporting any value from 1 to 120 minutes). Enable "All Tabs" to pause audio across every tab, or leave it off to only pause the current tab. The timer continues counting even if you close the popup.

Customizing presets

Customize the preset durations in Settings → Presets → Sleep Timer.

Why did I get a high volume warning?

When you boost volume above 100%, the extension shows a one-time warning: "High volume may damage hearing. Use carefully."

Why this appears

This appears because the extension can amplify audio up to 500% using audio processing (Tab Capture). The warning only shows once per session and won't appear again until you restart your browser.

Safety note

Prolonged listening at volumes above 100% can damage hearing — use the boost feature carefully.
